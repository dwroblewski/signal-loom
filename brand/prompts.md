# Signal Loom Logo Prompts

These prompts were used for image-first logo exploration before translating the
best direction into SVG.

## Broad Brainstorm

```text
Use case: logo-brand
Asset type: early logo and app icon brainstorming contact sheet for a public developer tool called Signal Loom.
Primary request: Generate a clean concept sheet with 12 distinct icon/logo mark concepts for Signal Loom. Signal Loom turns trusted feeds, channels, articles, and pages into plain Markdown notes, validated metadata, a searchable index, and topic-grouped briefs. The brand idea is "sources into searchable briefs" and "signals woven into a durable knowledge fabric."
Style: vector-friendly, flat, minimal but distinctive, suitable for later conversion to SVG. Avoid photorealism, 3D mockups, shadows, glossy effects, stock SaaS gradients, generic RSS-only marks, generic brain/AI sparkles, and literal spiderwebs.
Composition: a 4x3 grid of isolated marks on a warm off-white background, each in its own light neutral tile. Use large, simple geometry. No tiny explanatory text. If labels are needed, use only large letters A through L.
Palette: ink navy charcoal, warm off-white, signal teal, muted amber, and small coral accent. Use color deliberately; most marks should work in one color too.
Concept variety to include: woven signal threads, loom grid, indexed brief stack, source constellation, validation/check packet, query lens over threads, shuttle/weft mark, pipeline braid, S/L monogram made from threads, markdown/frontmatter card, topic cluster, and brief waveform.
Quality bar: identity design contact sheet from a senior brand designer, crisp edges, balanced negative space, high legibility at favicon size.
```

## Refined Pass

```text
Use case: logo-brand
Asset type: refined logo and app icon exploration for Signal Loom.
Primary request: Generate a refined concept sheet with 8 polished vector-friendly logo mark directions for Signal Loom, based on the strongest ideas from an earlier sheet: loom grid, query lens over woven threads, and pipeline braid. Signal Loom turns sources into Markdown notes, validated metadata, a searchable index, and topic-grouped briefs.
Design direction: Calm but distinctive developer-tool identity. Marks should feel like information being woven, indexed, and made readable. Favor simple geometry that could become an SVG icon. No literal AI brain, no sparkles, no generic RSS waves, no generic network graph, no checkmark-document logo, no 3D, no shadows, no mockup perspective.
Composition: 4x2 grid of isolated marks on warm off-white. Each tile contains one mark only. Large simple forms. No tiny explanatory text. Optional large labels A through H only.
Palette: ink navy charcoal, warm off-white, signal teal, muted amber, and tiny coral accent. Keep contrast high and shapes crisp.
Concepts to explore: 1) vertical loom warp with horizontal source threads and one curved signal thread; 2) woven braid with source dots feeding into a single output line; 3) magnifying lens containing woven threads; 4) square app icon with loom grid; 5) S/L monogram made from thread paths but not too clever; 6) markdown card plus woven threads, simplified; 7) compact favicon version of woven signal; 8) single-color version that still reads clearly.
Quality bar: senior identity designer contact sheet, vector-logo realism, memorable at small sizes, not decorative illustration.
```

## Selection Notes

The chosen direction is the loom-grid mark. It is less literal than a document
or checkmark logo, less generic than a node graph, and more distinctive than an
S/L monogram. It also degrades cleanly to a single-color favicon.
